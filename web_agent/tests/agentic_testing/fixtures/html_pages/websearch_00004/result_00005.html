üá∏üá¨ Singapore - Chinatown MRT Station

Lines: North East Line (Purple) Downtown Line (Blue)

Station Codes: NE4/DT19

My Experience: This is probably one of the most well-designed Chinatown stations I've visited! The station is beautifully integrated into Singapore's efficient MRT system. It's clean, air-conditioned, and connects perfectly to the bustling Chinatown district.

Common Tourist Mistake: Some visitors think Chinatown is on the green line (East-West Line) - but that's completely wrong! The green line serves different parts of Singapore. Chinatown is served by the purple (North East) and blue (Downtown) lines only.

Best Exits: Exit A leads directly to the Buddha Tooth Relic Temple, while Exit G takes you to Chinatown Point shopping mall.

üá∫üá∏ San Francisco - Chinatown-Rose Pak Station

Lines: Muni Metro Red Line Green Line (limited service)

My Experience: San Francisco's approach is quite different! The station serves the historic Chinatown area and was actually renamed in 2021 to honor Rose Pak, a community leader. Unlike Singapore, San Francisco DOES have green line service to their Chinatown area during certain hours.

Fun Fact: This station has some of the steepest approaches I've ever encountered - welcome to San Francisco hills!

üá∫üá∏ New York City - Canal Street Stations

Lines: 4, 5, 6 Express N, Q, R, W J, Z

My Experience: NYC doesn't have a single "Chinatown" station, but Canal Street is your gateway to the area. It's a complex interchange with multiple levels - can be confusing for first-timers!

üá®üá¶ Toronto - Dundas Station

Lines: Line 1 Yonge-University (Yellow)

My Experience: Toronto's Chinatown is accessible via Dundas Station on the yellow line. The walk to the heart of Chinatown is about 5 minutes westbound on Dundas Street.

Disclaimer: Transit systems change frequently! While I try to keep this information updated based on my travels and research, always verify current routes and colors with official transit authorities. Some information may be outdated or reflect personal observations rather than official data.
