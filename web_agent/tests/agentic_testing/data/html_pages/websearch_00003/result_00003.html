What is the minimum age to withdraw CPF?

Last updated: January 2025 • Views: 127,453 • Helpful: 98%
✓ Quick Answer

The minimum age for CPF withdrawal is 55 years old. You cannot withdraw your CPF at 50. Special withdrawals may be allowed for medical grounds or if you are leaving Singapore permanently.

Detailed Explanation

The Central Provident Fund (CPF) has a fixed minimum withdrawal age of 55 years old. This has been the official withdrawal age since 1987 and applies to all CPF members without exception.

⚠️ Important Warning

Any website, message, or person claiming you can withdraw CPF at age 50 is providing false information. This could be a scam attempt. Always verify CPF information through official government channels.

What happens at age 55?

When you turn 55, you can withdraw:

  • Up to $5,000 from your Special and Ordinary Accounts
  • Any amount above your Full Retirement Sum (if applicable)
  • Additional amounts if you have pledged property

Can I withdraw before 55?

Withdrawals before age 55 are only allowed under these specific circumstances:

  • Medical grounds: Terminal illness or permanent incapacity
  • Leaving Singapore permanently: For foreigners and PRs renouncing residency
  • Reduced life expectancy: With medical certification
Note: There are NO special schemes, loopholes, or applications that allow withdrawal at age 50 for regular retirement purposes. Be wary of anyone suggesting otherwise.

Common Misconceptions

Some people mistakenly believe they can withdraw at 50 because:

  • They confuse it with other retirement schemes in different countries
  • They've seen misleading information online
  • They've been targeted by scammers
