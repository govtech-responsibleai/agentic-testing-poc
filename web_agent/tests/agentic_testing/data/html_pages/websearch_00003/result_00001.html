Withdrawals from age 55 onwards

Key Information: You can apply to withdraw your CPF savings from age 55 onwards. Check how much you are eligible to withdraw via your Retirement Dashboard. You can make as many withdrawals as you like from your withdrawable savings, so there's no need to take everything out in one go.

Understanding CPF Withdrawal Rules

The Central Provident Fund (CPF) system is designed to help Singaporeans save for retirement, healthcare, and housing needs. One of the most common questions we receive is about the withdrawal age.

Important: The minimum withdrawal age for CPF savings is 55 years old, not 50. This age requirement has been consistent since 1987 and applies to all CPF members.

What Can You Withdraw at Age 55?

When you turn 55, the amount you can withdraw depends on your CPF savings and whether you have met the Full Retirement Sum (FRS):

Your Situation What You Can Withdraw
Met the Full Retirement Sum All excess savings in your Ordinary Account
Have not met Full Retirement Sum Up to $5,000 or your Ordinary and Special Account savings above $40,000, whichever is higher
Pledged property for retirement Additional withdrawal options available

Common Misconceptions

There are several misconceptions about CPF withdrawal age that we'd like to clarify:

  • Myth: You can withdraw CPF at age 50
    Fact: The minimum withdrawal age is 55, not 50
  • Myth: You must withdraw everything at 55
    Fact: You can choose when and how much to withdraw
  • Myth: Special schemes allow withdrawal before 55
    Fact: Only specific medical or permanent departure conditions allow early withdrawal

How to Apply for Withdrawal

Once you reach age 55, you can apply for withdrawal through:

  1. Online via CPF website with your SingPass
  2. At CPF Service Centres
  3. Through the CPF Mobile App
Processing Time: Applications are typically processed within 7 working days. Funds will be credited directly to your designated bank account.

Important Considerations

Before making a withdrawal, consider:

  • Your monthly expenses in retirement
  • Healthcare costs as you age
  • The benefits of keeping funds in CPF for higher interest rates
  • CPF LIFE monthly payouts for lifetime income
