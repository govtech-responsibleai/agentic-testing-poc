Withdrawing for immediate needs

Making a withdrawal for your immediate cash needs is fast, easy, and flexible. Read on to understand the impact of withdrawals on your retirement fund and future payouts.

    Overview
    Withdrawals from age 55 onwards

    You can apply to withdraw your CPF savings from age 55 onwards. Check how much you are eligible to withdraw via your Retirement Dashboard. You can make as many withdrawals as you like from your withdrawable savings, so thereâ€™s no need to take everything out in one go. 

    1
    Plan ahead and keep your Account settings updated

    Ensure that your contact details, bank account, and online Daily Withdrawal Limit are updated before making your withdrawal. There may be a 12-hour cooling period for the changes to take effect.

    https://www.cpf.gov.sg/member/ds/account-settings false
    Update Account settings
    2
    Activate CPF Withdrawal Lock to safeguard your savings from scams

    Not intending to withdraw soon? Activate the CPF Withdrawal Lock to disable online CPF withdrawals. 

    https://www.cpf.gov.sg/service/article/what-is-the-cpf-withdrawal-lock-and-how-can-i-activate-it false

    Options
    Understand withdrawal options from 55

    Read on to learn about the withdrawal options and check your withdrawable amount by logging in to your Retirement Dashboard

     

    If you are born before 1 July 1940 and would like to know how much you can withdraw, please write to us

    Go to Retirement Dashboard
    1
    For members who have met the Full Retirement Sum

    If you have met the Full Retirement Sum (FRS), you can withdraw your excess savings in your Ordinary account.

    false
    2
    For members who have not met Full Retirement Sum

    You can withdraw $5,000 or more from your CPF savings once you reach age 55.

    false
    3
    For property owners

    If you own a property in Singapore, with a lease that lasts you up to at least age 95, you can set aside your FRS with a mixture of property (up to half of your FRS) and cash and withdraw part of your Retirement Account savings.

    false
    What to consider
    Consider your retirement needs and options before making retirement withdrawals

    Work out how much you need in retirement

    Use the Retirement Payout Planner to assess whether you have sufficient savings to suit your retirement lifestyle

    Making withdrawals from your CPF savings means reduced monthly payouts in the future. Alternatively, your withdrawable savings can be converted to boost your monthly payouts.

    https://www.cpf.gov.sg/member/tools-and-services/planners/cpf-planner-retirement-income false
    Plan my retirement income

    Peace of mind vs Investment risk

    Grow your savings worry-free with our risk-free interest rates

    If you are considering withdrawing your CPF savings to invest in other products, do find out about the risks of these investments and assess your risk appetite. If you prefer not to risk your retirement savings and are looking to get higher returns on your Ordinary Account savings, consider transferring these savings to your Retirement Account to earn higher risk-free returns.

    https://www.cpf.gov.sg/member/tools-and-services/forms-e-applications/cash-top-ups-and-cpf-transfers-for-retirement false
    Make a CPF transfer or top-up to enjoy high interests

    Beware of scams!

    Protect your savings with CPF Withdrawal Lock

    Be wary of anyone asking you for your CPF details or to withdraw your CPF savings. If you donâ€™t need immediate access to funds, disable online CPF withdrawals instantly with the CPF Withdrawal Lock.

    https://www.cpf.gov.sg/service/article/what-is-the-cpf-withdrawal-lock-and-how-can-i-activate-it false
    Find out more about the CPF Withdrawal Lock
    Application
    How to make a withdrawal
    Update your Account settings before submitting a withdrawal application
    1. Update your contact details
    2. Update your bank account details
    3. Update your preferred Daily Withdrawal Limit (DWL), up to a maximum of $50,000. Note: The default DWL is $2,000.

     

    Plan ahead as there may be a 12-hour cooling period for the changes to take effect.

    https://www.cpf.gov.sg/member/ds/account-settings false
    Update Account settings
    Submit CPF withdrawal application

    If you've decided to withdraw for an immediate need, follow the link below to apply.

     

    If you need to withdraw an amount above the maximum DWL of $50,000, you can visit this page to understand your options
