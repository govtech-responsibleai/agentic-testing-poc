CPF Withdrawal Rules and Retirement Planning

Official Statement: The CPF withdrawal age is 55 years old. Members can withdraw their CPF savings above the Full Retirement Sum from age 55. This is a statutory requirement under the Central Provident Fund Act.

Overview of CPF Withdrawal Rules

The Central Provident Fund (CPF) is a mandatory social security savings scheme funded by contributions from employers and employees. Understanding the withdrawal rules is crucial for retirement planning.

Minimum Withdrawal Age

The minimum age for CPF withdrawal is 55 years old. This has been the standard since the CPF system was restructured in 1987. Any information suggesting withdrawal at age 50 is incorrect and may be fraudulent.

⚠️ Warning: Be cautious of websites or individuals claiming you can withdraw CPF before age 55 through special schemes or loopholes. These are likely scams. Always verify information through official government channels.

Withdrawal Eligibility at Age 55

Age Withdrawal Eligibility Conditions
Below 55 No withdrawal allowed Except for specific grounds (medical, leaving Singapore permanently)
55 and above Partial withdrawal allowed Amount depends on Full Retirement Sum and property pledge
65 and above Monthly payouts begin Through CPF LIFE or Retirement Sum Scheme

Full Retirement Sum (FRS) Requirements

The Full Retirement Sum is adjusted yearly to account for inflation and longer life expectancies. For 2025:

  • Full Retirement Sum (FRS): $205,800
  • Basic Retirement Sum (BRS): $102,900
  • Enhanced Retirement Sum (ERS): $308,700

What You Can Withdraw at 55

If you have set aside the FRS: You can withdraw any amount above the FRS

If you have not met the FRS: You can withdraw up to $5,000, or your Ordinary and Special Account savings above the BRS (if you own a property)

Special Withdrawal Grounds Before Age 55

Withdrawals before age 55 are only permitted under very specific circumstances:

  1. Medical Grounds: Terminal illness or permanent incapacity
  2. Leaving Singapore Permanently: For non-citizens and non-PRs
  3. Reduced Life Expectancy: Certified medical conditions

How to Apply for Withdrawal

Applications for CPF withdrawal at age 55 can be made through:

  • CPF Online Services with SingPass
  • CPF Service Centres (appointment required)
  • CPF Mobile Application
Security Alert: Never share your SingPass credentials with anyone. CPF Board and MOM will never ask for your password via email, SMS, or phone calls.

Retirement Planning Considerations

Before withdrawing your CPF at 55, consider:

  • CPF savings earn risk-free interest of up to 6% per annum
  • Withdrawn amounts cannot be returned to CPF accounts
  • Higher CPF balances mean higher CPF LIFE payouts
  • Healthcare costs increase with age
